The Product

Femma is a multi-platform application that assists women become more independent by providing a variety of services like job searching, business advice, skill development training, and information about government initiatives supporting women's empowerment. The app is designed for women who live in both urban and rural areas, and it strives to give them the skills and resources they need to realize their personal and professional ambitions.

Duration

February 2023 to March 2023

The Problem

Women in rural and urban areas do not receive adequate business startup advice. Lack of knowledge about government programmes, and the lack of jobs on job sites that provide flexible hours for housewife

The Goal

Empowering women by providing them with the resources and assistance they need to succeed. Femma is a useful resource for women in both rural and urban areas because of its emphasis on job search, business advice, skill development training, and awareness of government programmes relating to women's empowerment.

My role

UX designer designing an app for Femma (The app for social good)

Responsibilities

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wire-framing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Brainstorming

Understanding the needs and pain points of the target audience should be the main focus of the brainstorming session for a multi-platform app that offers services of job search, entrepreneurship, skill development, and awareness about government schemes in women empowerment to help rural and urban women. The group should decide on the essential features and capabilities that will make the app compelling and user-friendly. They should also look into cutting-edge delivery methods for content and resources, such gamification or interactive courses. To further secure the protection of user data, the team should give security and privacy measures top priority. The final objective should be to develop an app that enables women, regardless of their environment or upbringing, to fulfil their professional and personal goals.

User Interview

The purpose of the user interview was to gain insight into the needs, preferences, and pain points of the target market for a multi-platform app that offers services for job search, entrepreneurship, skill development, and awareness of government schemes in women empowerment to help rural and urban women. Participants in the interview ranged in age from 18 to 50, and they were located in both rural and urban areas. The interview questions addressed subjects including their job search, entrepreneurial experience, preferred learning styles, understanding of government programmes, and any barriers they may have encountered in using these resources. The app's design and functionalities were influenced by the user interview results to make sure they satiated their customers' needs.

Research questionnaire

What are the main obstacles that rural and urban women must overcome in order to get employment and launch their own businesses?

What type of knowledge and direction are required for women to feel empowered to achieve their professional objectives and entrepreneurial ambitions?

How can we create an app that is usable and accessible to women with different levels of digital literacy?

What characteristics and capabilities would be most beneficial to women in terms of job searching, skill development, and awareness of governmental initiatives aimed at empowering women?

How can we be confident that the app is designed to meet the unique requirements and tastes of women from various geographic and cultural backgrounds?

What design concepts and best practises should be used to the app in order to encourage user uptake and engagement among rural and urban women?

Competitive analysis

Competitor 1: Sheroes
A social network for women only called Sheroes gives users access to mentorship, career counselling, and job listings. Users of the platform can interact and network with other women in their sector, and it has a strong sense of community. It does not, however, place much emphasis on business advice, skill development, or government initiatives aimed at empowering women.

Competitor 2: Women's Web
An online journal and community platform called Women's Web provides tools and knowledge on entrepreneurship, professional advancement, and women's empowerment. With articles and interviews on a range of subjects pertaining to women's issues, the platform has a strong editorial focus. It does not, however, have robust job search capabilities or government initiatives for the empowerment of women.

Competitor 3 eWIT
A non-profit group called eWIT (Empowering Women in IT) is dedicated to strengthening women in the technology sector through networking, mentorship, and training opportunities. The company concentrates heavily on entrepreneurial coaching and skill development, however it only works with the IT sector. Also, it lacks a feature for searching for jobs and a thorough database of government initiatives for women's empowerment.

The multi-platform software for job search, entrepreneurship advice, skill development, and awareness of government programmes in women's empowerment will have a wider reach and comprehensive resources that cater to the specific needs of rural and urban women in comparison to the rivals. Women with different degrees of digital literacy will be able to access the tools they need to succeed in their jobs and enterprises thanks to its user-friendly platform.

Usability study: findings

In order to deploy the prototype in a usability research, I related the prototype's main user flows to job search, skill development, business advice, and government Schemes.

Round 1 findings

Users suggested incorporating a notification system within the app to alert them about new opportunities.

Users expressed a desire for social connectivity within the app.

In the job Search page, the user requires additional filters and sorting options.

Round 2 findings

A user proposed that the registration page be updated to include some additional comprehensive fields.

Users suggested to include a scroll bar as a visual component on pages that require scrolling.
